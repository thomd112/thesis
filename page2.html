History of Color

Colors have been a fundamental part of human history and culture. From the earliest cave paintings to modern design, colors have conveyed emotions, beliefs, and stories. Ancient civilizations like the Egyptians and Romans used natural pigments to create vibrant works of art and ceremonial clothing.

During the Renaissance, the development of new pigments and dyes expanded the artist's palette. Artists like Michelangelo and da Vinci used these to create some of the most iconic masterpieces in human history.

In modern times, the science of color has evolved dramatically. The discovery of synthetic dyes in the 19th century revolutionized industries from art to fashion. Today, colors are not only a visual experience but also a psychological and emotional tool in communication and design.
